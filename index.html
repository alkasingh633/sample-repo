A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missle Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.2 He was born into a poor Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15, 1931.02 Kalam started working at a young age to support his father, who was a boat owner and an imam of a local mosque. He spent more than forty years as a science administrator and scientist mainly at the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and Defence Research and the Development Organization (DRDO). Kalam played an important role in the second Pokhran nuclear test in 1998 and was associated with India's space program and missile development program.2 He was honored with several prestigious awards, including India's highest civilian honour "Bharat Ratna" in 1997.1
